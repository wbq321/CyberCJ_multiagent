Module 1: Introduction to Cybersecurity

1.2 Terminology

Basic Cybersecurity Terms:

  • Threat: Action that could cause harm.
  • Asset: Item with value (information in our case!).
  • Vulnerability: Weakness in security.
  • Risk: Exposure to danger.
  • Threat Agent: Person who could carry out the threat.
  • Attack Vector: How the attack could occur.

Risk Strategies:

  • Risk Avoidance: Avoid the risk in the first place, such as not storing information that could be stolen.
  • Risk Acceptance: Accept there's a risk and do nothing.
  • Risk Deterrence: Deter threat actors by advertising measures such as warnings and legal actions.
  • Risk Mitigation: Make the risk less serious, such as by storing information in separate locations.
  • Risk Transference: Hand the risk to someone else, such as by buying insurance.

Threat Agent Variants:

  • Cybercriminals: Seek fortune, not fame.
  • Script Kiddies: People who are in it for thrills and notoriety.
  • Brokers: Sell vulnerabilities to highest bidder.
  • Insiders: Retaliate against own organization.
  • Cyberterrorists: Seek to cause pain.
  • Hacktivists: People who think they are righting wrongs.
  • State Actors: Those who spy on and disrupt governments and citizens.
