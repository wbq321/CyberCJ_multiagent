Module 1: Introduction to Cybersecurity

1.1 Why are Computer Security, Internet Security, and Privacy Important?

Computer Security

Computer security is essential for safeguarding computer systems and their contents from unauthorized access and malicious attacks. It ensures the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of data, which is vitally important across every industry. Effective computer security helps maintain the operational continuity of systems and the protection of sensitive information.


Internet Security

Internet security is becoming more critical than ever, as nearly all organizational activities and communications depend on being connected online. This area focuses on protecting the data that flows across networks, ensuring that both the information and the underlying infrastructure are safe from various cyber threats. It's essential to maintain strong internet security to guard against online vulnerabilities that could have widespread and significant impacts.


Privacy

Privacy is a major concern because both personal and organizational data are often processed and stored electronically. Maintaining privacy standards means handling sensitive information carefully, respecting individuals' rights, and adhering to legal requirements. Good data management and strong privacy practices are essential to prevent unauthorized access and build trust in digital systems.
