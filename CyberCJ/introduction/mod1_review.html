CIA: The Three Protections

Confidentiality

Confidentiality

Only authorized parties should be able to view the information (example: credit card information).

Integrity

Integrity

Information should be free from tampering (example: amount of purchase).

Availability

Availability

The right people need to be able to access data as needed (example: security not so complex that merchant canâ€™t access order information).

AAA: Three Considerations

Authentication

Authentication

Ensures user is not an impostor (example: 2FA to log on)

Authorization

Authorization

Permission to use technology resources (example: clerk allowed to see order history for customer)

Accounting

Accounting

Tracks events (example: who accessed server when)

Basic Cybersecurity Terms

Threat

Threat

Actions that could cause harm.

Asset

Asset

An item that has value (information in our case!).

Vulnerability

Vulnerability

A weakness that can be exploited.

Risk

Risk

Exposure to danger.

Threat agent

Threat agent

A person who could carry out the threat.

Attack vector

Attack vector

How the attack could occur.

Risk Strategies

Risk avoidance

Risk avoidance

Avoid the risk in the first place, such as not storing information that could be stolen.

Risk Acceptance

Risk Acceptance

Accept that there's a risk and do nothing.

Risk Deterrence

Risk Deterrence

Deter threat actors by advertising measures such as warnings and legal actions

Risk Mitigation

Risk Mitigation

Make the risk less serious, such as by storing information in separate locations.

Risk Transference

Risk Transference

Transfer the risk to someone else, such as by purchasing insurance.

Types of Threat Actors

Cybercriminals

Cybercriminals

Exploit vulnerabilities to acquire wealth rather than to seek fame.

Script Kiddies

Script Kiddies

Exploit vulnerabilities for thrills and notoriety.

Brokers

Brokers

Sell vulnerabilities to the highest bidder.

Insiders

Insiders

Exploit vulnerabilities to retaliate against their own organization.

Cyberterrorists

Cyberterrorists

Exploit vulnerabilities to cause hardships.

Hacktivists

Hacktivists

Exploit vulnerabilities because they believe they are righting wrongs.

State Actors

State Actors

Spy on and disrupt governments and citizens.
