Module 4: Case Study

Carpenter v. United States (2018)

Background

The case of Carpenter v. United States (2018) set a significant precedent in the realm of digital privacy and Fourth Amendment protections. The Supreme Court ruled that the government must obtain a warrant before accessing an individual's cellphone location data, marking a pivotal shift in privacy law as it applies to the digital age.

Scenario

In 2011, Timothy Carpenter was implicated in a series of armed robberies. To build their case, the FBI obtained Carpenter's cellphone location data from his wireless carriers without a warrant. This data included 127 days' worth of Carpenter's movements, which the FBI used to place him near the crime scenes. Carpenter argued that this was a violation of his Fourth Amendment rights, which protect against unreasonable searches and seizures.

Supreme Court Ruling

In a landmark decision, the Supreme Court ruled 5-4 in favor of Carpenter, stating that accessing historical cellphone location data without a warrant constituted a search under the Fourth Amendment. The Court recognized the sensitivity and extensive nature of location data, which can reveal a detailed and intimate portrait of a person's life, including their movements and associations over time.

Key Takeaways

  1. Expectation of Privacy: The Court emphasized that individuals have a reasonable expectation of privacy in their physical movements and that this expectation is not nullified by the fact that the data is held by a third party (the cellphone company).


  2. Digital Age Considerations: The decision acknowledged that digital data presents unique privacy concerns that differ from those of the past. The Court stressed the need to adapt Fourth Amendment protections to the realities of the digital age.


  3. Warrant Requirements: The ruling established that law enforcement must obtain a warrant based on probable cause to access cellphone location records, reinforcing the need for judicial oversight in digital surveillance.

Implications

The Carpenter decision has broad implications for privacy rights in the digital age. It extends Fourth Amendment protections to various forms of digital data, setting a precedent for future cases involving technology and privacy. This case has influenced how courts handle other digital privacy issues, ensuring that privacy rights are safeguarded even as technology evolves.
