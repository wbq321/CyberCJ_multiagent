Module 4: Privacy

Module 1 Review

Data Theft

What is Data Theft?

What is Data Theft?

When information is collected and used without the knowledge or permission of the owner.

Data Theft Risks

Data Theft Risks

Identity theft, Financial loss, Reputation damage, Legal consequences, Emotional stress, Privacy invasion, Loss of intellectual property, Operational disruption, Unintentional data sharing, Targeted phishing attacks, Perpetual cybercrime.

Cryptography

What is Cryptography?

What is Cryptography?

The practice of securing communications to prevent unauthorized access by encrypting data into unintelligible ciphertext, and the decrypting with a key to recover teh original data.

Benefits of Cryptography

Benefits of Cryptography

Confidentiality, Integrity, Authentication, Non-repudiation, Access Control, Obfuscation, Secure Communication, E-commerce Security, Digital Certificates and Signatures, and Regulatory Compliance.

Types of Cryptography

Types of Cryptography

Symmetric-Key Cryptography, Asymmetric-Key Cryptography (Public Key Cryptography), Hash Functions.

Cryptoanalysis

Cryptoanalysis

The art and science of analyzing and breaking cryptographic schemes. Focuses on uncovering weaknesses in cryptographic algorithms to decrypt without a key.

Cryptoanalysis Methods

Cryptoanalysis Methods

Frequency Analysis, Known-Plaintext Attack, Chosen-Plaintext Attack, Differential Cryptanalysis, Side-Channel Attacks.

Protecting Data at Rest

Protecting Data at Rest

Encryption, Access Controls, Data Masking, Physical Security, Regular Audits and Monitoring, and Data Lifecycle Management.

Protecting Data in Transit

Protecting Data in Transit

Encryption, Secure Protocols, Authentication and Integrity Checks, Virtual Private Networks (VPNs), and End-to-End Encryption (E2EE).

End-to-end encryption (E2EE)

End-to-end encryption (E2EE)

A method of secure communication where data is encrypted on the sender's system or device and only the recipient can decrypt it.

Cookies

What are Cookies?

What are Cookies?

Data tokens that help websites remember information about your visit, making your next visit easier, and the site more useful to you.

Types of Cookies

Types of Cookies

Session Cookies, Persistent Cookies, First-Party Cookies, Third-Party Cookies.

Session Cookies

Session Cookies

Store information for maintaining your browsing session, such as login status or items in a shopping cart. Closing your browser deletes session cookies.

Persistent Cookies

Persistent Cookies

Remember your preferences and actions across a site to make your future visits more convenient.

First-Party Cookies

First-Party Cookies

Set by the website you're visiting, they help the site remember things like your login status, language preferences, and other settings.

Third-Party Cookies

Third-Party Cookies

Set by a domain other than the one you are visiting, they're often used for online advertising purposes.
