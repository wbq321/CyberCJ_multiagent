2.2.4 Ransonmware Prevention

Effectively preventing ransomware involves a strategic combination of disconnecting potential entry points and maintaining secure backups.

Disconnect and Isolate Devices

Disconnecting devices can significantly reduce the risk of ransomware infection.

  • Local and Networked Devices:
    Any device with a drive letter (C:\, D:\, etc.) or networked storage that syncs automatically poses a risk if compromised. Using air gaps, or physical separations, between computers and storage devices can be an effective deterrent.
  • USB and External Drives:
    Physically unplugging USB storage devices and unmounting secondary hard drives when not in use can make them invisible to ransomware attacks.
  • Network Shared Directories:
    Limiting access to these directories by requiring login credentials from specific user accounts adds an additional layer of security.
  • Cloud Storage:
    Turning off automatic synchronization and requiring manual login for cloud services decreases the vulnerability of cloud-stored data.

Backup Data

Backups are essential for recovery in the event of a ransomware attack.

  • Data Copies:
    Regularly updated backups should be stored in secure locations, isolated from the network. This prevents ransomware from accessing both primary and backup data, allowing for recovery without paying the ransom.
