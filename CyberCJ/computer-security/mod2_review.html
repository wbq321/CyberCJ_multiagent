Malware

Primary Actions

Primary Actions

The actions used to categorize malware. They include: Kidnap data, Eavesdrop, Masquerade, Serve as Launchpad, Sidestep

Kidnap Data

Kidnap Data

This type of malware specifically targets and restricts access to critical data, systems, or devices. Examples include Ransomware and Cryptomalware.

Eavesdropping

Eavesdrop

This type of malware captures and transmits user data without consent. Examples include Keyloggers and Spyware.

Masquerade

Masquerade

This type of malware impersonates legitimate software, deceiving users into installing it under false pretenses. Examples include Potentially Unwanted Programs (PUPs), Trojans, and Remote Access Trojans (RATs).

Serve as Launchpad

Serve as Launchpad

This type of malware hijacks infected devices to initiate additional cyberattacks. Examples include Viruses, Viruses, and Bots.

Sidestep

Sidestep

This type of malware is strategically crafted to circumvent or neutralize security protocols. Examples include Backdoors, Logic Bombs, and Rootkits.

Defending Against Malware

Malware Defense Strategies

Malware Defense Strategies

Regular Software Updates, Antivirus and Anti-malware Software, Monitor Firewalls, Training and Awareness, Backup and Recovery Plans

Security Updates

Security Updates

Automatic Updates, Service Updates, and Security Patches are analogous with replacing your door locks after untrusted individuals obtain copies of your keys.

Antimalware Software

Antimalware Software

Software that scans and monitors your system, examines files, detects and removes viruses, prevents intrusion, and classifies software in terms of risk.

Firewalls

Firewalls

First line of defense that inspects incoming and outgoing data. Personal firewalls perform the inspection for your device, while network firewalls perform the inspection where your internal network meets the internet.

Backup Plans

Backup Plans

Backup plans involve saving important data to external devices, mitigating the impact of ransomware and other events that prevent access to a system.

Isolating Devices

Isolating Devices

Reduces the chance of a deviec being compromosied be severing its connection to the outside world. This is analogous to placing important documents in a safety deposit box.

Incident Response

5 Key Areas of Incident Response

5 Key Areas of Incident Response

The 5 key areas are detection and analysis, containment, eradication and recovery, digital forensics, documentation.

Detection and Analysis

Detection and Analysis

Identifying signs of a security breach and assesing the scope to determine the impact.

Containment

Containment

Involves limiting the spread of a security breach and securing your systems to prevent further damage.

Eradication and Recovery

Eradication and Recovery

Involves completely removing the threat from your systems and restoring operations using backups.

Digital Forensics

Digital Forensics

Involves the investigation of cyber attacks, aiming to uncover how they were executed and by whom.

Documentation

Documentation

Involves creating detailed records of all actions taken and findings observed during and after a cybersecurity incident.
