Module 2: Computer Security

2.3 Incident Response

As members of a profession that grows more reliant on IT systems every day, it is vital to have a proper incident response strategy to alleviate the impacts of cyber threats. The goal of incident response is to manage the aftermath of security breaches and/or cyber-attacks. If effectively implemented it should be able to limit damage, reduce recovery time, and lower costs resulting from such events. The importance of an effective incident response cannot be overstatedâ€”it is essential for maintaining trust and ensuring operational continuity.

There are five key areas to plan for when considering your incident response strategy:

  • Detection and Analysis:
    Learn to identify signs of a security breach through simple detection techniques, such as unexpected pop-ups or slow system performance. Initial analysis helps assess the scope of the breach so its impact can be determined.
  • Containment Strategies:
    Consists of both short-term and long-term actions to quickly and effectively prevent further damage resulitng from the breach. The long-term strategies tie back into bolstering your defense against malware.
  • Eradication and Recovery:
    Know how to eliminate threats from the system with the help of malware removal tools, and restore operations by recovering data from backups.
  • Digital Forensics:
    Involves collecting and analyzing digital evidence to uncover how an attack happened. Basic forensic tools can provide valuable insights without requiring deep technical expertise.
  • Documentation:
    Proper documentation supports legal scenarios, insurance claims, and compliance with regulations. It includes detailed records of all actions taken from the detection to the recovery phases.
