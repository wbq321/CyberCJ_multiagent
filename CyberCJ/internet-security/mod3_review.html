Internet Layers

Surface Web

Easily accessible and searchable through standard search engines like Google or Bing

Deep Web

Not accessible through standard web browsers and often require specific software, configurations, or permissions to access

Dark Web

Hidden portion of the internet that requires specific software, configurations, or permissions to access and acts as a hub for criminal commerce

Internet Security Risks

Phishing

Fraudulent messages that appear to come from reputable sources but are designed to trick recipients into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal details

Spoofing

Involves forging the sender's email address to make it appear as if the message originates from a trusted source

Drive-By Downloads

Viewing a website that is already infected with malware will infect your computer. The attacker is exploiting the websites vulnerability and injecting it with harmful malicious content.

Malvertising

Random ads seen on popular websites are outsourced by the owner and not always monitored for malware that is in disguise

Email Risks

Refer to various threats and vulnerabilities associated with the use of email communication

Browser Dangers

Refer to various risks and vulnerabilities associated with web browsers, which are the primary tools people use to access the internet

DDoS Attack

Overwhelm a target system, network, or service with a flood of traffic from multiple sources, rendering it inaccessible to legitimate users

Zero-Day Exploits

Target vulnerabilities in software or hardware that are unknown to the vendor or have not been patched yet

Cyber Espionage

Involves the unauthorized acquisition of sensitive information or intellectual property for espionage purposes

Social Engineering Attacks

Manipulate human psychology to trick individuals into divulging sensitive information, performing actions, or compromising security

Internet Security Defense

Unautorized Attempts

Unexpected logins or access attempts to sensitive criminal justice databases or systems

Phishing Attempts

Emails or messages requesting sensitive information, such as login credentials or personal details

Security Alerts and Incident Reports

Notifications from security tools or incident reports highlighting unusual activities or potential security incidents

Compliance Violations

Instances of non-compliance with security policies, data protection regulations, or criminal justice standards

Unauthorized File Access

Instances of unauthorized access to confidential case files or evidence repositories

Suspicious Network Activity

Anomalies in network traffic, such as unusual data transfers or connections to suspicious IP addresses
